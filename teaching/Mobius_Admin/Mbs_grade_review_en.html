Revewing Grades

You must use force Grade on the assignment before modifying grades. If you don't do that, new grades will not be transferred to BrightSpace.

Introduction

You should not normally have to review the answers of the students. You should use the following procedure only to mark the questions where students had to upload a document. You may also want to look at the solutions given by the students to find out their common mistakes.

Procedure

  1. To review the grades given by MÃ¶bius to the assignments of your students, first select Class Grades under the item Gradebook of the top menu (item 1 of the figure below).

  2. You get a long form to do a search for the results of your students. The upper part of the form is displayed below.

    The first section is Select Lessons & Assignments. As the title suggests, you select the assignments for which you would like to get the results (item 2 in the figure above). The next section is Filter Results.

    • You may enter the ID number of a student in the box beside User (item 3 in the figure above) if you want only the results for this specific student.
    • You may request under Results (item 4 in the figure above) the results for all attempts, for only the best attempt, for all the attempts starting with the best one (the one selected in the figure), ... that the students have made for the selected assignments.
    • You may restrict further you search by requiring under Progress (item 5 of the figure above) all the attempts submitted only, those in progress only, or both of them.

  3. The bottom part of the form is displayed below.

    The third section is View Panel. There are many items that you may choose to be retrieved and displayed. We mention only those which are the most often used.

    • The first name and last name of the students (item 6 in the figure above), and their student ID (item 7 in the figure above)
    • The start date and end date (item 8 in the figure above), and start time and end time (item 9 in the figure above). This is often use to determine how much time students have spent on their assignments or, more likely, when they have started to work on their assignments.
    • The progress (item 10 in the figure above). It is often used to find out if the assignments are completed (submitted) or in progress. You may request both.
    • The Total Points for each student assignments (item 11 in the figure above). This information is always given independently of if you check or not the box.

  4. When you have complete the form, you may click on Search (item 12 in the figure above) to get a display that will look like the following one.

    • At the top of the table (item 13 in the figure above) is the list of pages that are displayed, identified by the first three letters of the last name of the first student listed on that page. You can click on these three-letter items to move from page to page.
    • There is a little icon in the column Progress (item 14 in the figure above) if the student has started the assignment. It is yellow if the assignment is in progress and green if the assignment has been submitted.
    • The grade for the assignment is in the column Grade (item 15 in the figure above). This grade is a link. If you click on this grade, you will get the full details of the student's assignment.

  5. After clicking on the student grade for an assignment, you get a long page with the detailed solutions of the assignment for this student. We display below the top of such a page.

    The top section of the page has the grade that the student has obtained for the assignment (item 16 in the figure above) and general information about the student's performance. Under this section is the list of all the questions of the assignment.

  6. We display one of the questions below.

    The answer given by the student is on the left. The right answer is on the right. The border of the box with the student's solution is green if the answer is right. It is red if the answer is wrong.

    There are several methods to modify the grade of an assignment.

    • YThe first method is to use the box beside update selected grades as (item 17 in the figure before the one above) to type in the grade that you want to attribute to all the questions that you have previously selected (item 17.5 in the figure above). You have to press Update Grades (item 17 in the figure before the one above) to implement the modifications. I do not see any reason to modify the grade of many questions at the same time.
    • The second method is to modify the full grade of a question (item 18 in the figure above), ignoring the results for the components of the question. You can click on the icon for comments (item 18.5 in the figure above) to add the reason for changing the grade.

    I will focus the explanation to the last method (sitem 19 in the figure above) to modify the grade of an assignment. This is the one that I recommend because it is closely associated to the reason to modify the grade.

  7. If you click on the icon beside the Grade for a component of the question (item 19 in the figure above), you get the following display.

    You enter the new grade for this component of the question in the box beside Grade (item 20 in the figure above) and press the icon that looks like a floppy disk (item 21 in the figure above) to save the modification. If you change your mind, you can always cancel the operation by clicking on the X icon (item 22 in the figure above).

If you go back to the list of assignments using the back arrow of your browser, you may realize that the grade of the assignment has not changed. You need to refresh the screen to see the new grade. If you are in the process of reviewing many assignments, you should not bother with refreshing the screen and just move on to the next student.
