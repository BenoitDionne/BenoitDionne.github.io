Adding Modules to Your Course

Normally, you start with an empty page. The screenshot below contains many modules and sub-modules (Chapter 1, Section 1.1, Fichier PDF, etc) that were previously added by the author.

Suppose that you want to add a module entitled chapitre 2. it suffices to type in the title of the module in the little field under the Table of Contents in the left menu, and press return. You get the following left menu

and the following content on the right side of it.

You now have to define the properties of your new module.

  1. Click on Add dates and restrictions ... (item 1 in the figure above) to set the period of accessibility of the module. You may also give conditions that students must satisfy to access the module. By default, there is no restriction at all on accessing the module. This is probably fine for most modules.

  2. If you click on the item Add a description ... (item 2 in the figure above), you get the following display where you can enter the description of the module.

  3. You can select under Upload / Create (item 3 in the figure at the top of the list) the type of document that you want to add to your module.

    You may Upload Files, Create a Link, etc. This menu is really important in general but not for us right now in the context of linking BrightSpace to Möbius. We will not say more about it.

  4. There are several activities under Add Activities, also called Existing Activities (item 4 in the figure at the top of the list) that you can include to a module. The most important one for us is External Learning Tools. We show in , and how we use it.

  5. You can select Bulk Edit (item 5 in the figure at the top of the list) if you want to edit all the properties of a module at the same time. We will not cover this item in this document since we have never used it.

  6. This area Drop and drag ... (item 6 in the figure at the top of the list) is for those who do not like menu. You can simply drag and drop a document into this area to add it to the module. After that, you can proceed with editing the property of your document as we are now doing for the module that we are now adding.

  7. You can use Add sub-module,... (item 7 in the figure at the top of the list) to add modules inside a module. It suffices to write the title of this sub-module in this field. For instance, if you type in chapitre 2.1, we get the following left menu

    and the following content in the right area.

    If you click on the title section 2.1 in the Table of Contents, you get the full menu to edit this new sub-module.

    You edit this sub-module as we are doing right now with the module chapitre 2.

  8. If you click on Published (or Unpublished) (item 8 in the figure at the top of the list), you get a menu that let you unpublish (or publish) a module, a sub-module or any document.

    In the latest version of BrightSpace, this item is represented by an eye for visible and an eye that has been stroked through for invisible. When you create a module, the default is Published.

  9. Finally, there is a drop-down menu under the title of the module (item 9 in the figure at the top of the list).

    There are some useful items under this drop-down menu:

    • You can edit the title of the module with Edit Title (item a in the figure above).
    • Select Download (item b in the figure above) to download the documents that you may have uploaded to the module. You get a zip file of these documents. WARNING: this does not do the backup of the module.
    • Use the Set Default Path (item b in the figure above) to choose the directory on the BrightSpace server where the documents that you upload to the module will be stored.
    • You can always use Delete Module (item d in the figure above) to do exactly that, delete the module.

    The items Move Up and Move Down may be useful but it is more efficient to drag and drop modules to move them.

There is a sophisticated graphic tool to add content to your course. The item Course Builder under Course Admin gives you access to a graphical interface to add modules, documents, ... to your course. It is easy to use this tool if you learned what we presented above.

Managing Grades

Introduction

After selecting the tab Grades in the top menu of the website for your course in BrightSpace, select the tab Manage Grades to get the following display.

There are two items present on that tab by default: Final Calculated Grade and Final Adjusted Grade which are not displayed in the figure above. We address these items in the section below. For now, we consider the two items, Item and Category, under the cascading menu New (item 1 in the figure above).

You select Item (item 2 in the figure above) to create a component to evaluate the students' knowledge; for instance, the final exam, a partial exam, an assignment, etc. You select Category (item 3 in the figure above) to create a components of your evaluation that is composed of many items; for instance, the category Assignments may contains the items: Assignment 1, Assignment 2, ...

Create a Category

If you select Category, you get the following display.

Initially, you are under the Properties tab (item 4 in the figure above). You enter the title of the category in the General section (item 4.5 in the figure above). Let's say that this category will be called Midterm. There are two important items in the section Grading (item 5 in the figure above). In the present example, we assume that you have selected Weighted for your Grading System when you created your course on BrightSpace. You enter the percentage of the final grade associated to this category in the field Weight (item 5 in the figure above).

You select under Distribution (item 6 in the figure above) how the items included in the category contribute to the grade for this category. You can select to (1) manually assign the contribution of each item to the grade for this category, (2) have all items assigned the same weight, or (3) to have the weight of the items determined by the maximal grade associated to the item (e.g. an assignment out of 20 will count for two assignments out of 10).

When you are done, you must Save (item 7 in the figure above) your work before going to the second tab; namely, to the Restrictions tab given below.

The must useful items are found in the General section (item 10 in the figure above) of this tab. They are Category is always visible and Hide this Category. By selecting the latest item, you hide the results for this category from the students.

When you have finished, you can Save and Close (item 11 in the figure above) if you do not have to define another category, or you can Save and New (item 12 in the figure above) if you want to define another category.

Create an Item

If you select Item (item 2 of the first figure of this section), you get the following display. An item can be an assignment, an exam, ... To simplify the discussion, let's assume that you want to add an assignment.

Let's assume that you use numerical values to grade your students. If you select Numeric (item 13 in the figure above), you get the following display.

As for the General section of the form to create a category, the General section (item 14 in the figure above) has fields to enter the title of the assignment that you are creating. Let's say that the title of your assignment is Assignment 4. There is a field that was not present for the definition of a category. It is a field to assign the assignment to a category. Let's say that you have already created a category Assignments. Then, you will select this category under the pull down menu associated to the field Category (item 15 in the figure above). There is also a link to create a New Category if you have not done it before. After selecting the category Assignments (item 20 in the figure below), you should note that the item Weight (item 17 of the figures above and below) in the section Grading is no longer editable.

This is expected because if the category Assignments as been defined such that the weight of an assignment in this category is determined by the maximal grade associated to this assignment.

If the assignment that we are creating is to be included in a category where you manually assign the contribution of each assignment to the grade for this category, then you enter the percentage of the grade for this category attributed to this assignment.

If this assignment is not included in any category, then you enter the percentage of the final grade attributed to this assignment.

You use the drop down menu under the item Grade Scheme (item 18 in the figure before the previous one) to select how grades will be displayed to the students. You have two options percentage and uOttawa Alpha. You will more likely choose the first option for assignments from Möbius.

The Restrictions tab is almost identical to the Restrictions tab under the definition of a category. The only difference is that category is replaced by item in the menu. For instance, you select the item Hide this Item to hide the results for the assignment from the students.

For those serious educator, there is an Objectives tag where you can write the learning outcomes that students should reach by completing this assignment.

Entering Grades

Introduction

First select the tab Grades in the top menu of your course website in BrightSpace. Then, select the tab Enter Grades if it is not the one automatically selected. You get the following display.

There are many items to consider in this display. The purpose of the items Import (item 1 in the figure above) and Export (item 2 in the figure above) is clear. you will click on them to import grades from a spreadsheet to the grade book of BrightSpace and to export the grades from the grade book on BrightSpace to a spreadsheet.

You can use the field under item 4 in the figure above to request the results of a particular student. You press the magnifying glass beside this field to start the research. This is useful if you are looking for the results of one student in a class with hundreds of students.

We will talk about the button Switch to Spreadsheet View (item 5 in the figure above) in a moment when talking about entering grades. You can select some or all students, and press the Email icon (item 6 in the figure above) to send them an email.

Note that the little arrow under item 7 in the figure above (a similar arrow may appear on the left if needed) let you scroll the grade book when it is wider then the browser display. Some versions of BrightSpace don't have this little arrow.

The cascading menu More Actions (item 3 in the figure above) offer some additional options (but not that many more).

You select Hide/Show Columns to select the categories and items (columns of the grade book) that you want to see.

You simply check the box beside the items that you want to see, and press Save when you are done. The results under the non-selected category or item are not hidden from the students. To do so, you have to use the Restrictions tab when you define a category or an item as explained in the section above.

Entering Grades

There are two options to enter the grades for an item. If you select Grade All from the drop down menu that you get by clicking on the little down arrow beside the name of the item,

you get the following display.

In this presentation, the student ID numbers have been removed from the table. Consult the section above to find out how to add the student ID number beside each student name.

You enter grades as you normally do in a spreadsheet in the section identified as item 6 in the figure above. Please, do not forget to save the data that you enter on a regular basis. You do not want to loose all your work because you inadvertently close your browser.

  • If you have many students who got the same mark, you may select all of them by checking the box beside their name and clicking on the icon Grade (item 1 in the figure above) to get a popup menu that let you type in the mark they all have.

  • Similarly, if you need to clear the mark of many students, you select all of them by checking the box beside their name and clicking on the icon Clear (item 2 in the figure preceding this list) to get a popup window asking you to confirm your action.

  • If a student is exempted to do an assignment, a partial exam, ..., then you can remove this item from the computation of the final grade by checking the box beside the name of the student and clicking on the Exempt icon (item 3 in the figure before this list). If the item is part of a category, the computation of the grade for this category is done as it should be done if the grading distribution is Distribute weights by points across all items in the category or Distribute weight evenly across all items. It is not clear how the grade is computed in other situations. More to come on this subject in the near future; namely, if someone wants to add something more.

  • To cancel the exemption that a student may have for an item, check the box beside the name of the student and click on the Unexempt icon (item 4 in the figure before this list)

  • The role of the Email icon (item 5 in the figure before this list) is to send emails to all the students who would have been selected.

Please, never forget to Save and Close (item 7 in the figure before the list) when you are done. In particular, before closing your browser or shutting down your computer.

The second option to enter grades is to click on Switch to Spreadsheet View (item 5 in the figure before the list) to get a normal spreadsheet.

There is no options in this mode. You can go back to the default view mode by clicking on Switch to Standard View (item 8 in the figure above). Please, never forget to Save (item 7 in the figure above) your work on a regular basis.

Finally, if you want to enter or edit the grade for several items of a student, you can simply click on the name of the student to get the following display.

Interpreting the Grades

You have probably noted that each student gets three values separated by commas for each item. The first value is the mark of the student for the item. The second value is the ratio of the number of points that the student gets over the number of points that the item represents for the final grade. Finally, the last value is the grade that the student gets out of 100. This last value is determined by the choice that you made for the item Grade Scheme when you created the item. There are also colours associated to the mark of the students. In the present case, blue is associate to the students who got a grade of 90% or higher.
